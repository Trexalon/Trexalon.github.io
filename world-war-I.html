World War I


Introduction

World War I is the first world war in history. It started after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand that took place at Sarajevo, Bosnia. Archduke Franz Ferdinand was assassinated by Gavrilo Princip. Archduke Franz Ferdinand was assassinated in June 28, 1914.

Location

World War I was fought across Europe, mainly on two sides, the western front and the eastern front. The western front was held on a long line of trenches that ran from the cost of Belguim to Switzerland. The majority of the fighting took place in France and Belguim. The eastern front wasfought in Russia and Romania (of The allied and Associated Powers) versus the Central Powers countries of Germany, Austria-Hungray, and Bulgaria.

Teams

The teams of World War I were the Central Powers (mainly Germany, Austria-Hungray, and Turkey) against the Allies(mainly France, Russia, Italy, Japan, and, from 1917, the United States).
